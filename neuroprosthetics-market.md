<p><p>The neuroprosthetics market is divided into two main types: output and input neuroprosthetics. Output neuroprosthetics include devices that interface with the nervous system to restore motor functions, such as prosthetic limbs or spinal cord stimulators, enabling movement and improving quality of life. Input neuroprosthetics, on the other hand, involve devices that receive neural signals to assist with sensory functions, like cochlear implants for hearing or retinal implants for vision. Both types aim to enhance human capabilities and address neurological impairments.</p></p>
